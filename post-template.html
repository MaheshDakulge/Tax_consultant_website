New vs Old Tax Regime in FY 2025-26

A comprehensive mathematical breakdown and decision framework by J. R. Jinke, FCA.

Insight
Income Tax 22 May 2025 J. R. Jinke, FCA 8 Min Read

The Budget has made the New Tax Regime the default choice for all tax filers in India. If you wish to opt for the Old Regime, you must actively declare it. But default doesn't mean optimal. Let's compare both options mathematically.

For decades, tax filing in India was synonymous with tax saving — buying insurance, investing in PPF, and setting up home loans to maximize deductions. The introduction of the simplified New Tax Regime altered this approach by trading deductions for lower basic rates.

The Mathematical Baseline

To understand the difference, let's examine the structure of both regimes side-by-side for the Financial Year 2025-26 (Assessment Year 2026-27):

Tax Slab New Regime Rate Old Regime Rate
Up to ₹2.5 Lakh Nil Nil
₹2.5 Lakh – ₹3.0 Lakh Nil 5%
₹3.0 Lakh – ₹5.0 Lakh 5% (Rebate available) 5% (Rebate available)
₹5.0 Lakh – ₹7.0 Lakh 5% (Rebate available) 20%
₹7.0 Lakh – ₹10.0 Lakh 10% 20%
₹10.0 Lakh – ₹12.0 Lakh 15% 30%
₹12.0 Lakh – ₹15.0 Lakh 20% 30%
Above ₹15.0 Lakh 30% 30%

The Break-Even Concept

The choice between the two regimes comes down to one single question: How many deductions are you claiming?

If you have high deductions (under Section 80C, 80D, HRA, and Home Loan interest under Section 24), the Old Regime might still yield a lower tax liability. If you do not have significant investments or housing expenses, the New Regime is almost certainly the better choice.

"As a rule of thumb, if your gross annual income is ₹15 Lakh, you require at least ₹3.75 Lakh in eligible deductions for the Old Regime to match the tax liability of the New Regime. If your deductions are less than that, the New Regime wins."

Standard Deduction Updates

Salaried individuals benefit from a standard deduction of ₹75,000 under the New Regime (increased from ₹50,000) and ₹50,000 under the Old Regime. This change shifts the break-even math slightly in favor of the New Regime, making it even more attractive for mid-to-high salaried professionals.

How to Decide: A Simple Checklist

  • Compute your total gross income from all sources.
  • Add up all your deductible investments (PPF, EPF, ELSS, Insurance premiums).
  • Calculate your house rent allowances (HRA) or home loan interest payments.
  • Use our online Income Tax Calculator to compare the exact estimates.

Ultimately, a structured, professional evaluation is highly recommended before the financial year ends. Moving assets into lock-ins like ELSS or PPF just for taxes can compromise liquidity and investment returns if it doesn't yield a net tax benefit.
